Star Trek: Phoenix
IKS NI'RAH / prelude
Captain Syrezeaju 17-Jan-21 07:26 PM
[D] Dimly lit corridors were accented with red lighting in typical Klingon fashion. It did nothing to conceal the old metalwork and how here and there even just the corridors clearly showed the ship's age. The exterior - that took the brunt of the battles it had waged for almost seventy years - likely looked even worse up close. From afar, however, the nI'raH was as impressive as it was when it had first set out on its maiden voyage - the sister ship of the fleet's flagship, the same class, and one of the Empire's sturdiest ships. It was along these corridors that three figures now walked, the first two leading the third. Clad in ceremonial armour fit for war, it seemed as if the rumours that had been heard by the third might well be true - that this was a Klingon vessel to be sent into a Federation war. Confirmation had not yet been given, though. Confirmation had not been given to the third about anything, leaving them clueless of why they were there. The CO of this ship may well have been an old friend but that did nothing to warrant a trip out to the vessel itself - nor a welcome by two senior escorts dressed in their full finery. It was seemingly a mystery, and hopefully one about to be solved upon entering the doors to the end of this short corridor, the one guarded by another two statue-still officers in ceremonial garb.
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 07:49 PM
Rashen walked quietly as the trio swept through the corridors. The environment was still, the dim halls abandoned. That was unusual. Where was the loud ring of laughter? The friendly war cries of crew mates sparring? There wasn't any sign of life aboard the ship, not a footfall, not a breath. The soft, low hum of engines had even disappeared. They seemed the only living beings aboard. And he did not know these two warriors walking by his side. It was unnerving. It put him on edge. Something was about to happen, something weighty was going to be revealed, he knew it in his gut.
Captain Syrezeaju 17-Jan-21 08:16 PM
[D] As the three approached the two other officers stood aside, holding back bat'leths instead of attempting to block their path. The escorts, in turn, also stood aside. The door was motioned to as the four stood out of the way - a clear indication for Rashen to proceed. The quarters aboard Klingon vessels were nowhere near as opulent as those aboard Starfleet's own ships. The hulking design of the ship's hull may have suggested that it was built for space but it was nowhere near as roomy as anticipated by outsiders; all of that space was taken up by older-style engineering that they had been loathe to replace. The same could be said for the captain's quarters. Masses of space was instead condensed down to one long room - and these quarters in particular were adorned with ceremonial blades and trinkets; it would have been heaven for a collector of Klingon antiques. How strange that was, considering that the occupant of this room was a Nausicaan. The man in question was at the far end of the elongated room. Dim lighting almost concealed him but the sound did not. Click, inhale, click, exhale, click- over and over again, loud, mechanised, and definitely not done by the person themselves. Lain upon a Klingon hospital bed of hard metal, with the back partially raised, he was still dressed as if ready to go into battle. Shoulder pads were laced with metal panels befitting of the rank, gauntlets were the same - and draped over him was a cloak, frayed at the bottom and washed out over the years, but it was much more befitting than some blanket was. His head was turned to the side. Eyes were unseeing but still they stared ahead, waiting for the sound of the doors which would announce the arrival of his old friend.
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 08:28 PM
Rashen paused before entering the room, taking a couple deep breaths to settle his nerves. He then stepped inside. The room was like a display of history, and Rashen read it like an open page. The man who lived here was a warrior of the highest calibre. The ornate weaponry displayed on the walls were a testament to his honor. A shadow of Rashen passed through their polished blades as he walked by them towards the back of the room. He stopped a respectable distance away from the bedside, allowing for the man to keep his privacy, if he so wished. The harsh machination of the respirator grated in his ears. He had heard news of the condition of his old friend. This was a moment he had dreaded for a long time. The sight before him was enough to break the heart of a warrior.
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 08:34 PM
Waiting felt like it took an eternity - an eternity he would just have to keep waiting, the flag officer had told himself, as he was not going to put command of this ship into anyone else's hands. The first officer was good, as was the second, and third, but... With the news of being sent onwards to join the Federation in their war, he needed someone with the right experience. Someone he could trust - and someone his crew could trust. Someone who would ensure they could find glory in battle but also give them a fighting chance. He propped himself up on both elbows at the sound of heavy footsteps - new Klingon boots always clicked against these floors. One arm was immediately outstretched, beckoning him to come closer. The voice was soft, tired, nowhere near the gruff and commanding bark it once had been. It would have been drowned out by the respirator's continuous tone had it not been for the relief carried upon the words. "Rashen, my friend, please... Come closer. It is you, isn't it..? I was certain that you wouldn't ignore my summons..."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 08:39 PM
"I've come, old friend." Rashen came into the shadows directly to the bedside, taking the frail, outstretched hand.
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 08:47 PM
The grasp upon Rashen's own hand was surprisingly strong, swiftly taken as if in the worry that he suddenly might disappear. It had been too long waiting, too long. "I began to worry when it... Took you some time. I thought you must be busy. And then, I thought, how busy can he be if he is awaiting a new starship?" He barked out a laugh - the result was a singular, almost choking sound. "But - I bring you good news, my friend. You don't need to wait... You can take nI'raH. She is yours."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 08:57 PM
Rashen was not at all surprised by the power of Dash's grip, even now. He had seen this man in his prime, when it took three of the strongest warriors present to hold him down. He may have grown heavy with years, but his strength had not yet left him. "I will turn her bow to glory." He said softly, "And of you, Dash?"
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 09:05 PM
"Of me?" The laugh sounded legitimate despite the circumstances. You could have imagined him shaking his head as he contemplated how silly the question was. "I am no longer a warrior. I have been a burden to this crew ever since that poison took hold. How I only wish that I had been able to kill Torghn myself..." The vision of revenge played well in his mind for just a moment, earning a flicker of a smile, before it faded into something far more solemn. Beady eyes which had clouded over somehow made exact eye-contact with Rashen's own. "No. It is time for me to go."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 09:12 PM
"Your crew will not take well to that. They wanted to see you wreak your vengeance." Rashen replied, "I... would have liked to see it as well." He held the dark gaze steadily. Anger flared up inside him, but it was swiftly quelled by a deep sadness. He knew that what Dash was saying was right. But it wasn't right that he'd been laid low by a coward. It wasn't right that the coward was not to be cut down by Dasjhan's blade.
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 09:29 PM
"Many people would have liked to see Torghn torn apart for his crimes," he mused quietly, "but some things are not to be. And that is simply the way life goes, it seems. ...I believe you know what I wish to ask of you, my friend. I will understand completely if you cannot help."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 09:34 PM
"I will do as you ask, my friend." Rashen said evenly, though soreness was creeping up his throat. A dark pit filled his stomach.
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 09:44 PM
The repetition did not go unmissed, and despite having been preparing for this for all of the time he had waited, that statement being reiterated back to him still seemed to hit home. 'My friend.' "There is a blade - the Nausicaan one - careful, for it is sharp... I did not think that the more traditional of this crew would be pleased if a Nausicaan undertook the Hegh'bat with a Klingon blade," Dash chuckled out in some sort of hacking noise. "But still... That blade belonged to the first Nausicaan to ever serve on the High Council. They only lasted one day, mind you, before being thrown out by the real Klingons... But I feel as if it is still an honour in itself."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 09:52 PM
Rashen moved to survey the wall. He picked up a velvet cloth from a small chest nearby and lifted the weapon from its rack by the blade, the velvet wrapped about his hands to protect them. It was a thing of beauty, simple though it be. He gazed into his own eyes that were mirrored on the polished metal. He brought it back to the bedside slowly. "There is honor in his courage to face the High Council, despite knowing their opinions."
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 09:59 PM
His hand remained outstretched, awaiting the hilt of the blade. "And I hope that there is honour in what I am about to do. ...A warrior's death for what I hope was a warrior's life, that is." There was silence for a moment. "Rashen... You must know what comes next for this crew. Their orders are to join the Federation in war, escorting one of their ships. An attempt to make up for all we did in the last year, I believe. ...But this war is unlike one we have seen for many years - not since this ship first sailed. It is being waged against the Iconians, Rashen. Death awaits us all -- but I hope it does not find its way to this crew so soon..."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 10:07 PM
Rashen placed the hilt of the blade un his open palm, but he held on to the blade for a moment, preventing him from raising it just yet. "Dasjhan, you know your crew far better than I perhaps ever shall. You know that they will not die lying down. Some of them may find their way to Stovokor. Some of them will live on because of the other's sacrifices. But the crew of the nI'Rah will survive, whole and intact. Their spirits are too stubborn to die." He said this while looking his friend directly in the eye.
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 10:14 PM
"And I only hope that your spirit shall be the same, my friend. Lead them well - and lead them for a long time," he stated with an affirming nod as fingers of cold hands wrapped around the hilt of the blade. "You have the same fire that they do. They'll approve of you..."
HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 10:20 PM
He took a deep breath and released the blade. His eyes were dry, but the soreness in the back of his throat had grown. He looked at Dasjhan with a soft expression. A part of him wished that time would suddenly stop, and he could linger here with his friend, frozen forever. But it was wrong to wish that. Now was the time to say goodbye, and it would last only a few moments longer. "I will see you again, dear friend."
Sa'Hom Dasjhan BOT 17-Jan-21 10:36 PM
"In Sto-vo-kor, I hope," came his reply - whispery voice stronger than it had been for the duration of the conversation. With hands which were surprisingly steady - as they should have been for he had been a warrior, once upon a time - he traced the blade over the fabric of the cloak until the slight indent marked where was required. The tip pierced through the thick fabric. "Live well, my friend," he stated to Rashen - and in one sharp movement, hands tugged inwards to bring the blade through his heart.

HoD Rashen BOT 17-Jan-21 10:57 PM
Rashen stood silent for a long moment. He closed his eyes, willing his tears not to come. They did anyway. Shaking his head slowly, as if that would make them go away, he uttered a prayer for Dasjhan to quickly find his way to Sto-vo-kor. He bent down and pressed his forehead to his friend's for a moment. A tear slipped from his cheek and fell onto Dasjhan's. Then he removed the blade from his friend's chest and very meticulously wiped the blood from it onto his right sleeve. This completed the ritual. He cleaned the blade so that it once again shone with deadly clarity, and he returned it to its rack carefully. He took a deep breath to calm himself. His tears dissolved back into his eyes. He lifted his head high and walked out into the hall where the honor guard was waiting.
