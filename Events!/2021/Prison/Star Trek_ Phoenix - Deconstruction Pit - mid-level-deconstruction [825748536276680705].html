Lt. JG. Haitek Maleb 03-Apr-21 05:16 AM
He didn't know how long it had been when he decided he'd been on the ground for far too many... minutes? Hours? Like Commander Yllescas put it, the work had to be done. The pain from being poked and dragged and dropped had mostly abated and faded, and Maleb - though feeling the setback of losing more blood, his body colder than before and his heart still beating uncomfortably quickly - knew, instinctively, that he had to move. Staying down when hurt was dangerous. One bit at a time and he could get up. Planting his right hand on the ground, the hybrid made himself roll into his back - yes, he used the arm on his injured side, and had to twist around that part of his torso, but it meant that he wouldn't have to put weight on the wound itself. The tradeoff was logical, even if Maleb didn't consciously make the decision. Now on his back, he could stop to breathe before pushing into a sitting-up (well, propped up on his elbow) position with his stronger arm; he nearly fell back in the process, but the hybrid persevered. The next part, though, standing up? How was going to manage that? Everything blurred together as Maleb used his core to get into a position where standing up was even physically feasible - the only reason he didn't scream was because a most of his limited capacity for intentional thinking was going to remaining stoically silent. No, standing wasn't an option. He would actually pass out, or at least do further damage. Calling for a medic wasn't an option, either - they'd almost certainly take him back to his cell, only for the cycle to repeat over again. Was there some sort of quota on work? Probably. That seemed logical. He had to work. He had to make standing an option. So he did. He repeated to himself his mantra of, 'deep breaths' and stood all the way up, leaning against a wall as he bordered on blacking out and his vision went fuzzy and dulled. But he held on, just barely, slumping against boxes of supplies. Maleb wasn't giving up yet.
