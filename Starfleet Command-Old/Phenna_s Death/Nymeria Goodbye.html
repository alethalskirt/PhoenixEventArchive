It had been a month, since the passing of her friend. There had been times where Nymeria would sit there in her office and ponder on whether on not her spirit was still out there. Her mind was still conflicted, as she found it hard to believe that a Q had the ability to die, even though Phenna was only a percentage of Q. She struggled to cope with the loss, dwelling on the past conflicts with her and finding all the negativity and the wrongs that Nymeria herself had put onto the Fleet Admiral. The young lady finally had to do something about this, a confession perhaps. One that she had been wanting to do for a very long time, but her own stubbornness and arrogance kept her from admitting it. Nymeria planned this visit weeks in advance, she had been working on something rather special for Phenna before she had passed, however, when she did, Nymeria felt more guilt than she had ever felt before. She found herself washing up on the shores that lead her to the place she was travelling to. The place seemed to be bare, the shores rocky and wavy, especially for the season. The breeze was cold, yet calming, Nymeria found herself bundled up in a nice fur coat, the outer lining, protecting her from the bite of the cold breeze mentioned before. France expected her trip to be easy, however when she found herself traversing through the unkept path, her steadiness began to fumble.
With her arms laid out in a T position, she balanced herself, trying to equalize her weight as she walked through the mush and slush of the snow that had been caked onto it. Nymeria had been careful with the contents that she was currently holding, her grip was tight, yet not too tight, as the package she was carrying was rather delicate. She looked over to the right, once she had reached the point where she wanted to stop, and saw a worn down cottage or hut of some sort. The wood was tearing apart at the seams, however, it valiantly stood its ground, almost like a statue not wanting to fall during a storm because it lived for a day that it could see the view of the sunset on a cool summers day. France stood there, thinking about it, scanning it with her eyes, then looking behind her to find the view that the cottage was trying to find. A sigh came out of her mouth, which formed a cloud of steam that dissipated with ease, blowing in the direction of the glorious tree. Speckled with beautiful autumn colors, as if it was still fueled with the nutrients to push forward. Like a fire that wouldn't cool as it had been too hot for it to burn out by the winds.
Nymeria stood in awe, the tree was a spectacle, however she wasn't here for the tree. The tree, in a sense, was not her mission. It was the plaque that was nailed onto the tree, weared down slightly by age, but refined with a new name attached onto it. Like someone was trying to edit it. Her name, shined, Phenna…. Ephrix. No matter what kind of weather came on this island, her name would still shine on the plaque that was dedicated for her, and the others she held dear. Maybe it was because of her name, that the tree before Nymeria became vibrant. Almost like the plaque in front of her, was a hug of life that Phenna always enjoyed, even though she might not have shown it. Smiling, Nymeria walked forward, admiring the plaque once more, before cleaning it up, revealing the other names, Seann and James. Satisfied with her caretaking, Nymeria sat down and sighed, before looking out to the sea and closing her eyes in thought.

Maybe, maybe you know where they have gone. Or maybe you sent them back home. You were always one to care for anybody, no matter how irri-.” She paused, remembering the past that she once was in. Nymeria remembered the arguments between the Admiral and Nymeria, the amount of times where Nymeria had wanted to say sorry but Phenna could never hear her. The look of fear and regret was in Nymeria's eyes, as one tear fell down her face, soon, becoming frozen onto her cheeks in the bitter cold.

Nymeria wanted to do something for Phenna, to make up for all her mistakes, but on the day that she planned to give it to her, Phenna had died. I just hope now, that you are in a good place. Resting among the stars with your true family, a home…. That was made for you.” She said, crying, all of her memories flooding through her mind as she put her forehead to the tree. Trying to deliver some kind of message, a memory perhaps, but Nymeria would never know if it worked. Instead she would just stay there and kneel down to the ground.
