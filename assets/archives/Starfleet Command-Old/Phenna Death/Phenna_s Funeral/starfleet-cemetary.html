Commander Dunamis 07-Nov-20 05:50 PM
The silence that followed was deafening. Even without the gift of empathy or telepathy in any form, Dune could practically taste the sorrow and grief in the air. He saw it in the faces of the crowd. Some simply sad, some calm bordering crying, all mourning. He was sure that the same was present on his own as well. Nonetheless there was no more time to watch the crowd. They expected something of him - and it was time to deliver. With PADD set out in front of him on the podium - and, admittedly, a deep breath drawn in, he began:
"Dear colleagues, friends, fellow officers of Starfleet. Today we mourn the passing of Fleet Admiral Phenna Ephrix - former Surgeon-General of Starfleet Medical, counsellor and friend to all. When one thinks of her, a few words remain front and center of one’s thoughts: kind, humble, loving, caring, warm, understanding, patient. Traits which she had and exhibited in abundance - and then some. She was a veritable embodiment of those values, and that remained an ever-apparent fact, right up til the day of her death. For her there was no one unworthy of her love, kindness or her help - only that they were in need, and she was only glad to give of herself. All of us present here know that she gave generously of herself to others; of her time, love, attention and care - right up til the day of her passing. Even in the twilight days of her life, she chose to do what she did best: to help others and bring them life and light. Others might have thought to do otherwise, but she chose to give the last moments of her life to others. For that, we salute her."
I myself have had her counsel several times. In each and every encounter, all the traits I spoke about were readily apparent. Never once did she fail to remind me that I am not alone as I walk this path in life - that there are others who share the journey with me always. My most poignant encounter, however, took place just a few months ago. He paused here for a moment, feeling a ball of emotion expand in his throat and making it feel tight and dry. "It had been a month after a particularly stressful mission. I was recuperating in Starfleet Medical - lost, feeling hopeless, thinking of leaving this part of my life behind. My intentions were, for the better, not to be. She visited me then and helped me re-find my direction. She showed me that there remained hope over the horizon - and to grasp it, I first needed to run towards it with my hands, mind and heart open. She helped me to become who I am today, to relight the fire that kept me going before - and for that I shall ever be in her debt. Words alone cannot adequately pay tribute to her life or her legacy, for they are so great, so beautiful and so fully lived as to be truly singular. I would even call them legendary. We all know that there will not be another Phenna Ephrix. Therefore, the greatest tribute that we can give, together, is in deed. To be kind, to care, to love and to support those around us, and in so doing keep her memory alive. To be Starfleet, as it should be. A portion of what she put into the universe around her now returned. I believe that is all she would have asked for from us in return, if at all, even after a life spent giving so much and asking for nothing in return."
"If she can hear us now, and I believe in some way she can - Admiral, on behalf of the crew of the U.S.S. Phoenix, let me take this moment to convey our deepest, sincerest gratitude. Words cannot express how grateful we are to you for all that you have done for us. All those who you have helped and who have known you are gathered here today to pay their respects in these precious final few moments - and the outpouring of love, gratitude and sorrow from the rest of the fleet says it all. Rest in peace, Phenna, knowing that you leave behind a universe, a Starfleet and a Federation greatly changed for the better by your actions. We are all in your debt. May you find what you could not in life, in the existence that awaits beyond ours." He paused there, stifling a sob that built up in the base of his throat. Just a few more words. Come now. Finish off and you can handle the rest out of sight of the others. He took a deep breath, steeling himself for the closure. "Until we meet again, among the stars." Following which, he gave a choked 'thank you' to the crowd, and stepped off the podium, feeling his throat tighten as he joined the crowd below. Don't cry. Don't you dare cry. Not here and not now. You have a crew that needs you.
