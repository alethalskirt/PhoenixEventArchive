SI'. w'DI. Cherow 17-Apr-21 10:16 PM
The bar, like the rest of the ship, had been left in ruin. It was hard to tell where the bar began and ended, really, considering how one of the walls towards the hallway had been folded over and mangled. Tables (or what was left of them, as numerous had been broken entirely) were strewn here and there in the section that must have been the remainder of the open seating area. The chandelier which had once hung from the ceiling in a rather pretentious way of spicing the place up and making it not seem like the bar upon a starship had wound up on the floor, splintered into countless pieces. Shards of crystal glass had been scattered upon its impact to the ground. In the glimmers of light which shone in through areas of the hull which had been pierced, it created a cascade of rainbows here and there where it interacted with the shattered glass. The glass was not only from the chandelier, of course, but also from the numerous broken bottles which had not survived the crash. Some smart people out there chose reinforced bottles which would withstand something like this, knowing that a ship getting into a skirmish could result in a heavy loss of merchandise. Others were either too lazy to invest in such a design - or they were too pretentious, claiming that it spoiled the drink somehow. What did spoil the drink was its bottle being smashed and the contents winding up on the floor - what a bloody waste.
It seemed as if that had happened to a fair portion of the bartender's merchandise here, considering how the place smelled so strongly of the alcohol which had dripped down into the carpeted flooring. There was the dark scent of berries, plum - red wine, a Human favourite. Beneath it was the strangely bold, syrupy smell of Ferengi rum - so rarely made, and such a waste. Now and again if you inhaled deeply the scent of something more clinical and sharp which burned at the back of your nostrils and throat. Most would have thought it would be too clinical to drink, but some species would prove you otherwise and down it in one. Amidst the rubble - lamenting the loss of the merchandise - was not the bartender, surprisingly. The figure was too tall to be a Ferengi and the mess of grey-white hair - currently tied back - certainly confirmed otherwise. They appeared to have navigated to the area which was once the store behind the bar's main section. The glimmers of light from the discarded glass illuminated a silhouette and caught off a further bottle of alcohol, this one being held up to the nearest source of light itself as the figure squinted at the charred label. "Not telling me what you are does not help anyone," a low voice grumbled with displeasure as the person tried to make out the writing. "How may bets that the Ferengi would have watered you down, hm? No help to anyone at all..."

Having held his position of holding the bottle up to the light in an attempt to work out what it was (the stance would have seemed analytical and intelligent if it hadn't been for the circumstances, just give him a turtleneck and a pair of glasses and he would have looked the part), he opted to slowly lower the bottle this time instead. The deep, Nausicaan-accented voice carried surprisingly well despite the distance. "Can I help you, Starfleet?"

Starfleet may have been strangers to him but there was no use in being distant either; if the past two weeks had shown anything it was that people needed to band together. It was as such that he did listen with a feigned interest to begin with - but that interest became real at the early mention of 'own personal collection.' So the fellow was a connoisseur! Why hadn't they started with that? Tearing his gaze away from the bottle of absinthe - watered down but he could salvage that, it was practically calling his name - he looked to the Cardassian intently. "Springwine can be salvaged in a good cocktail, if you know what you're doing with it. It can become awfully sweet if you're not careful with it. ...And what I would not give to share in that kanar, Starfleet - if sharing is something you endorse," he then added with a hint of what may have been amusement. It was difficult to tell, really. "My own collection went down with the nI'raH. A tragedy, really..." And just like that, the amusement had vanished. Either the Nausicaan was unstable or perhaps already drunk. "Such a waste."
