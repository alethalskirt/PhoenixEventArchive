He had been frustrated at his pupils, he had wanted to tear out his hair when his daughter didn't contribute to housework or when Harsharn had made a complete tip of the place and refused to clean up... But he had never been driven to the point where he was frustrated to the verge of tears. He should not be working right now. He wasn't even supposed to have seen the message to the counselling team considering it had went to his office, not to his quarters - and his office was somewhere he was not supposed to be. He hated this starship and he hated Starfleet and he hated the Federation - and yet he was still working. Why? Because his colleagues had seemingly vanished into thin air, that was why. He hadn't seen them in about two weeks. For the remainder of their time at DS22 he had remained hidden away in his quarters. There had been a couple of offers of a friendly chat and the likes but did he want to talk to anybody? No. He wanted to talk to Vryoti who he had finally gotten a message to only to be blocked by her friends on comms platforms too. He wanted to talk with Haar who was gone. Talking had been his job and now he hated it... But he was still going to do it. He wasn't even in the right mindset and you had to be, but there was no other option because no one else was bloody going to. Coming to a halt outside of Lieutenant Maggie Roode's quarters he hit the door chime and stood back, expression impassive. He had read over the case notes and knew that this was not going to be an easy one to handle but he desperately preyed that somehow, Roode was managing to cope by himself. He couldn't deal with this. He couldn't fucking deal with this. It had better go easily or a bloody trip out of the airlock was going to seem very tempting; for both him and for his service user.
