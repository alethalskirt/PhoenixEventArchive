Lt. Cmdr. Dev Clark 20-Feb-21 07:39 AM
It seemed there was no break for the diplomat throughout the week-- considering two new small people were now filling his residency. With all the mayhem and the distribution antibiotic treatments, he caught word of the children the away team brought back. Children displaced by war. It hadn't been the first time he witnessed it. He had first had his run in with it when he worked during Acamar with the USS Mackey. He saw as child after child was lost from their parents and clearly remembers watching his fellow crew-men work desperately to find their parents once more. It was always a mournful time whenever they realized there was no parent for them to back to. It was because of that and the fact that his work allowed him to stay in his quarters that he originally offered to look after the two Cardassian children: Vell and Navar Elat. It had been the least he could do to relieve the stress of the medical staff and if he were honest? He couldn't bare the fact that they must've been terrified and without someone to comfort them during this time. For the good part of the week, they were quiet and hesitant to even approach him. But that didn't stop him from attempting to bring some sort of joy to them, anything to make the time not horrible. Whether it had been speaking to them in Kardasi or replicating different Cardassian foods in an attempt to find whichever oness they liked. Even in the short time they've been together, Kunzo slowly found small things they enjoyed. Navar would shy away from him if ever started a conversation but he found that if he left a couple of books out for her -- he'd find them gone in a few minutes when he wasn't looking. As for Vell, the boy was similar to his sister in the way that he shied away from him and wouldn't approach him. Though, as the week slowly went on, he found that the little boy would follow him around his quarters and would silently ask if he could go to Kun's office with him.

Unfortunately, upon their return to Earth-- he was told that their parents were MIA and in other terms, most likely deceased. Divines... he hadn't been trained for the moment he had to sit them down and explain to the two that the couple had gone to the stars. He was grateful for being very aware and somewhat versed in the different religions of Cardassia, especially North Kardasi religion. If he could have taken their pain as his, he would have. He didn't expect to find himself embracing two sobbing children that day but then again, he didn't expect a lot of what that week had in store for him. But of all emotions, he was shocked. Shocked at the maturity level between the two. Such a level that made his brows furrow. No child, no matter the situation should be ask to be anything more than a child. It hurt knowing that they had become so desensitized to these things. The night that he told them, they ended up preparing a small prayer shrine. Although he was not a follower of their religion, he would be doing them a disservice if he didn't at least try. They had no photos of the Elat couple but it didn't matter. Kun still set up the small shrine for them to kneel around. With the help of the replicator, they made a small feast - as one normally would in a formal service for all the guests. But instead, it was just the three of them in the quiet of his quarters; the only light coming from incense candles. It was odd the... attachment he had grown over the week. For a strange reason, he saw himself in both of them. No, he couldn't explain exactly what that meant to himself but it was just was such a strong feeling that he couldn't turn away. When the SFCPS contacted him with that he wanted to do with the two, he realized it wasn't decision. He waited a few days before asking what the siblings wanted to do. Kun made it very clear that, they absolutely had a home here for the time being but was sure to explain the foster care system the Federation had.
Frankly, he was a little shocked to hear Navar's firm response of wanting to stay here. Of course, while Vell was older, he was certainly a follower as he was quick to agree with his sister. The action caused a bright smile to appear on Kun's face. A smile that hadn't shown itself in much time. He was more than overjoyed to contact the SFCPS that he wanted to continue to foster the pair. No, he wasn't ready to adopt them and surely they weren't ready to be adopted either. He was a fair believer that the trio still had a long way to go and that maybe he just might be the right person for that. While his experience in being a father or more generally a guardian to any form of kin was near zero, he was sure as Hell willing to try if it meant he could see these kids happy for the rest of their lives-- as happy as they even could be. So yes, there were a couple of changes to his quarters. The room once used for as a mock trial room was now replaced by a bunk bed and a pretty hefty bookcase. He may or may not have cashed in on a favor with a certain Captain to acquire some Klingon history books along with a multitude of other novels. The diplomat was more than willing to indulge in Navar's interest in physical books. As for Vell, it was a little more difficult to find little things that he enjoyed. But if the boy found joy in joining him on his trips down to the Diplomatic corps. HQ, then so be it. As for himself, he had been fairly swamped with the aftermath of the war-- desperately working on the legal matters of this all. By no means was he dreading the work, in fact, he adored putting his effort into this. Things were a bit easier considering he had the basics of Iconian down. Speaking of which, he had certainly taken advantage of the language documents Dunamis had provided him and was relishing in the Kelpien language. While it was on the back-burner currently, he was definitely looking forward for finishing the first vocab PDF. (edited)
