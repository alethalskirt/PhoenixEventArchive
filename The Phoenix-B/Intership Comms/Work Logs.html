Acting commanding officer's log, stardate 154127.53. I regret to preface this entry with the likely notion that the Federation and Starfleet might never be the same. Not in the eyes of our cadets, not in the eyes of those who would join our numbers and certainly not in the eyes of our allies - no doubt having heard of the events that transpired on this day, the Chancellor, Proconsul and Great Link must be shaking their heads. I shall not assign blame, regardless of what the cause is. I am reminded starkly of the danger of powerlust. Phoenix's flame is dim. The crew is demoralized, having experienced loss far, far greater than I would ever wish on anyone in their shoes and so early on in their careers. But make no mistake: we will in time once more leap anew from the ashes, with fire brighter and stronger than ever before, and into the stars we shall soar, setting the stars alight. Junior Science Officer’s Log Stardate: 154987.72 Subject: Neutronium Analysis and Research Report Neutronium, known only to be found at the core of a collapsing star, is an extremely rare element. Starfleet has not managed to successfully produce the alloy as of yet, but there have been several instances of neutronium production and usage by other races and unknown groups over time. Perhaps the most well known (and topically relevant as of the current time) is by the Iconians, particularly during the Iconian war. However, it has also been known to be in use by the Dominion and in the discovery of the only known Dyson Sphere by the USS Enterprise-D*. In this report, I will be focusing on the use of neutronium by Iconians as a means to discuss ways it may be either counteracted, obtained, or even destroyed for the purposes of our current tactical developments in relation to the supposed future Iconian conflict. Neutronium consists only of neutrons; this makes the element highly radioactive. It is most easily collected in gaseous form, then processed through the use of high gravity manipulation (also known as antigravity). As mentioned earlier in this report, is only found at the core of a neutron star. Finding a star suitable for the production of neutronium, however, is no easy feat- said star must have a mass much larger than the mass of the Earth’s Sun, and in order to produce an sizeable quantity of neutronium, it must have an iron core of no less than 1.4 but no more than 3 solar masses**- if the core is denser than the maximum amount, it will surpass the supernova stage and instead collapse into a black hole. It is also important to note that neutronium cannot exist outside of the pressures of a neutron star- this is why it must be collected in gaseous form and compressed using antigravity.
 (edited)
Now that the creation of neutronium has been discussed, it is important to move on to the second facet of this report- how it may be possible to obtain, defend against, and/or destroy neutronium. Through my research, I have discovered that the containment of neutronium is hypothetically possible with a modified version of technology Starfleet has possessed for generations. The containment of neutronium could be achieved by the use of gravimetric spatial distortion. A precise use of gravitons to construct a strong enough gravitational field would contain the neutronium, stopping it from dispersing into the surrounding environment due to its lack of charge and electrostatic forces, however the field itself must also be contained; the effect of such a high gravitational distortion would affect anything making contact with it that cannot handle the pressure. As for the use and/or destruction of neutronium, far less research exists. Neutronium is known to be impervious to the current phaser and torpedo iterations in use by Starfleet, however this does not necessarily indicate that the element is wholly indestructible. As of yet, the only known way to destroy a ship or object made of neutronium is to force it to implode. However, there is another aspect of neutronium that may be useful in finding another way to cause a detrimental reaction to the element. Since neutronium can exist in a solid state only inside an immense pressure similar to that of a neutron star, it is possible that some sort of graviton distortion is in use by the races currently known to have produced the element. As mentioned earlier, however, this must be a contained distortion. Unless the Iconians or Dominion members are impervious to the immense pressures of the core of a neutron star, it can be assumed that if they have successfully contained the neutronium with a field similar to the one previously described, they have also contained the field itself.
It is my belief that if either or both of these fields can be disrupted or destroyed, that a) the neutronium will disperse into its surroundings (if only its containment field is removed), or b) the gravitational field containing the neutronium will cause such distortions in the surrounding areas that no nearby organisms will be able to survive (if only the field around the graviton field is removed).
